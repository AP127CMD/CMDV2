## Phase I — Basic General Handling
*14:00 hrs | 13 lessons | All Dual | Day VFR | Local*

| # | Lesson Code | Duration | Condition | Dual/Solo | Lesson Title | Objective |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| 1 | `CDGL 01` | 1:00 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | First Air Experience & Familiarization | To provide the trainee with an initial exposure to flight in an aeroplane and to develop basic familiarity with the aircraft environment, flight sensations, and the role of controls. |
| 2 | `CDGL 02` | 1:00 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Effect of Controls | To introduce the trainee to the primary flight controls and their effects on the aircraft's movement around the three axes. |
| 3 | `CDGL 03` | 1:00 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Standard and Medium Turns | To develop the trainee’s ability to maintain straight-and-level flight and perform standard and medium bank angle turns in both directions. | 4 | `CDGL 04` | 1:00 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Climb and Descent | To develop the trainee’s ability to perform climbs and descents at constant airspeed on a constant heading, including during coordinated turns. |
| 5 | `CDGL 05` | 1:15 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Turns in Various Configurations | To develop the trainee’s ability to perform coordinated turns under different aircraft configurations, including climb, descent, and approach settings. |
| 6 | `CDGL 06` | 1:15 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Basic UPRT (1) — Arrest Divergence / Avoid Spin | To develop the trainee’s ability to recognize and respond to undesired aircraft states through timely and appropriate control inputs to prevent loss of control in flight. |
| 7 | `CDGL 07` | 1:15 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Basic UPRT (2) — Flight Path Management | To develop the trainee’s ability to maintain control and awareness of aircraft behavior during steep turns, slow flight, and high-speed flight. |
| 8 | `CDGL 08` | 1:15 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Basic UPRT (3) — Stall Recognition & Recovery | To train the trainee to recognize and recover from stall events in various flight configurations relevant to different flight phases. |
| 9 | `CDGL 09` | 1:15 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Simulated Forced Landing | To train the trainee to manage an engine failure emergency and carry out a safe simulated forced landing using proper procedures and judgment. |
| 10 | `CDGL 10` | 1:00 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Circuit, Take-Off and Landing (1) | To train the trainee to perform a normal circuit with standard take-off and landing procedures, and to recognize and respond appropriately to a rejected take-off at a reasonable speed. |
| 11 | `CDGL 11` | 1:00 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Circuit, Take-Off and Landing (2) | To develop the trainee’s ability to perform standard circuit procedures and approach techniques, including normal and flapless landings, with consideration of wind effect, aircraft configuration, and the correct execution of a go-around. |
| 12 | `CDGL 12` | 1:00 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Circuit, Take-Off and Landing (3) | To train the trainee to handle abnormal situations in the circuit, including simulated engine failure during and after take-off, approach and landing with idle power, and executing a go-around. |
| 13 | `CDGL 13` | 0:45 hr | Day VFR / Local | Dual | Final Pre-Solo | To assess the trainee’s proficiency and confidence in conducting safe and independent circuit operations in preparation for the first solo flight. |
